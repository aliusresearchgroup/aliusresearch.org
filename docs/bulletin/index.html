The irruption theory: How mind and matter meet along the route to consciousness

Tom Froese, interviewed by Matthieu Koroma

Issue 7Tom FroeseConsciousnessPsychedelicsNeuroscienceMethods

Abstract

In this interview, Tom Froese and Matthieu Koroma address the irruption theory, an account of how mental actions manifest themselves as unexplained variability in brain and bodily activity. The discussion considers how this approach can trace consciousness in physiological signatures such as scale-free activity and informational complexity, and how altered states can help disentangle the physiological correlates of consciousness from agency.

Speaker biography

Tom Froese is a cognitive scientist whose work connects embodied cognition, complex systems, agency, consciousness, and altered states.
