\AliusQuestion{What is the purpose of this template project?}

\AliusAnswer{The goal is to let contributors focus on content rather than formatting. Editors and interviewers can open a piece folder, fill in the title, abstract, DOI line, questions, answers, pull quotes, and references, and rely on the shared class file to preserve the standard ALIUS look.}

\AliusParagraph{\lipsum[1]}

\AliusPullQuote{Contributors should be editing content, not rebuilding layout by hand.}

\AliusQuestion{How should memorable quotes be inserted?}

\AliusAnswer{Each pull quote should be placed near the answer passage it comes from. In practice, that means adding a `\AliusPullQuote{...}` block immediately after the answer paragraph that contains the quoted material or after the closest relevant answer subsection.}

\AliusQuestion{How does the project support full bulletin assembly?}

\AliusAnswer{The issue-level build collects multiple piece folders into one bulletin. The issue front matter provides the title page and contents page, and each piece keeps its own metadata and body file. This gives the project a nested hierarchy: individual interviews are authored locally, while the full issue is assembled centrally.}
